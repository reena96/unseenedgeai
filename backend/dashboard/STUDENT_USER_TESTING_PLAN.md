# Student Portal User Testing Plan

## Overview
This document outlines the user testing plan for the UnseenEdge AI Student Portal to validate that middle school students (grades 6-8) can understand their skill assessments, feel motivated by the growth-oriented feedback, and navigate the portal independently.

## Testing Objectives
1. Verify students can understand their skill profiles (target: 85%+ comprehension)
2. Confirm growth-oriented language is encouraging and not discouraging
3. Validate accessibility features work for students with diverse needs
4. Assess mobile responsiveness on devices students actually use
5. Measure student engagement and motivation after viewing their data

## Target Participants
- **Number**: 10-15 middle school students
- **Grades**: 6-8 (ages 11-14)
- **Diversity**: Varied reading levels, tech comfort, and demographics
- **Special Considerations**: Include 2-3 students with accessibility needs (e.g., screen reader users, high contrast needs)

## Test Environment
- **Portal Version**: Student Portal v1.0 (Streamlit)
- **Devices**: Mix of desktop computers, tablets, and smartphones
- **Access Method**: School computer lab + personal devices (with parental consent)
- **Duration**: 20-30 minutes per student
- **Setting**: One-on-one sessions in comfortable, non-judgmental environment

## Testing Materials

### Pre-Test Materials
- Parental consent forms (privacy, recording, participation)
- Student assent forms (age-appropriate language)
- Welcome script explaining the purpose
- Sample login credentials

### Post-Test Materials
- Emoji-based satisfaction survey (visual, age-appropriate)
- Interview questions with simple language
- Gift card or small thank-you item

## Test Scenarios

### Scenario 1: First Login and Orientation (3 minutes)
**Task**: Login and look around the portal
**Success Criteria**:
- Logs in independently
- Notices the welcome message
- Can describe what the portal is for

**Observation Points**:
- Does the student seem comfortable or intimidated?
- Is the interface visually appealing to them?
- Do they notice the navigation elements?

**Questions**:
- "What do you think this website is for?"
- "Does it look friendly or confusing?"

---

### Scenario 2: Understanding the Skill Web (5 minutes)
**Task**: Look at the radar chart and explain what it shows
**Success Criteria**:
- Can identify their strongest skill
- Can identify their skill that needs work
- Understands that bigger = better

**Observation Points**:
- Can they read the skill names?
- Do they understand the shape of the web?
- Do they connect it to their own experiences?

**Questions**:
- "What does this spiderweb chart show about you?"
- "Which skill are you best at? How can you tell?"
- "Does this match how you see yourself?"

---

### Scenario 3: Reading Growth Feedback (5 minutes)
**Task**: Read the feedback messages for each skill
**Success Criteria**:
- Understands the feedback is about them
- Can explain in their own words what the feedback means
- Feels the feedback is positive and encouraging

**Observation Points**:
- Do they read all the feedback or just skim?
- Do they smile or seem engaged?
- Do any messages confuse or upset them?

**Questions**:
- "How does the feedback make you feel?"
- "Is there anything you don't understand?"
- "Does it feel like the website believes in you?"

---

### Scenario 4: Exploring Achievement Badges (5 minutes)
**Task**: Look at the badges they've earned and describe what they mean
**Success Criteria**:
- Notices the badge section
- Understands badges are achievements
- Can explain why they earned specific badges

**Observation Points**:
- Do they feel proud of their badges?
- Do they understand the criteria?
- Do they want to earn more badges?

**Questions**:
- "What are these badges for?"
- "Are you proud of any of these? Which ones?"
- "Would you try to earn more badges?"

---

### Scenario 5: Viewing Progress Over Time (5 minutes)
**Task**: Look at the progress timeline and describe what they notice
**Success Criteria**:
- Understands the graph shows change over time
- Can see if skills are improving
- Feels encouraged by visible growth

**Observation Points**:
- Can they read the timeline graph?
- Do they understand the concept of progress?
- Does seeing growth motivate them?

**Questions**:
- "What does this graph show?"
- "Are your skills getting better? How can you tell?"
- "How does it feel to see your progress?"

---

### Scenario 6: Writing a Reflection (5 minutes)
**Task**: Write a short reflection about their skills
**Success Criteria**:
- Understands the purpose of reflection
- Feels comfortable writing
- Knows who will see it (them + teacher)

**Observation Points**:
- Do they write freely or hesitate?
- Is the tone positive or negative?
- Do they understand privacy (not public)?

**Questions**:
- "Would you actually use this? Why or why not?"
- "Do you feel safe writing here?"

---

### Scenario 7: Mobile Experience (if time permits)
**Task**: View the portal on a smartphone or tablet
**Success Criteria**:
- All features are accessible on mobile
- Text is readable without zooming
- Buttons are tappable

**Observation Points**:
- Does the layout adapt well?
- Can they navigate easily?
- Do they prefer mobile or desktop?

## Comprehension Assessment

After testing, assess student understanding with simple questions:

1. **Basic Comprehension** (Yes/No questions):
   - "Does the portal show your skills?"
   - "Are the scores about how good you are at each skill?"
   - "Is the feedback trying to help you grow?"

2. **Deeper Understanding** (Short answer):
   - "Tell me in your own words what this portal does."
   - "Which skill do you want to work on? Why?"
   - "Would you want to check this portal again in a few weeks?"

**Target**: 85%+ correct on basic questions, 70%+ on deeper questions

## Ethical Considerations

### Privacy
- No identifying information in recordings or reports
- Students referred to by ID numbers only
- Reflections remain confidential

### Emotional Safety
- Stop testing immediately if student becomes upset
- Frame all feedback as "helping us improve," not testing the student
- Allow students to skip questions or tasks
- Emphasize there are no wrong answers

### Parental Rights
- Parents can review materials beforehand
- Parents can withdraw consent at any time
- Parents receive summary of findings (no individual results)
